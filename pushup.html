Push-Up Guide
Step 1: Set Up Properly

Start in a plank position with your hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Ensure your body forms a straight line from head to heels, engaging your core.

Step 2: Hand Placement

Position your hands directly under your shoulders, fingers pointing forward. Keep your wrists in line with your shoulders to maintain proper alignment.

Step 3: Body Position

Distribute your weight evenly between your hands and toes. Squeeze your glutes and brace your core to maintain a stable body position.

Step 4: Elbow Position

As you lower your body, keep your elbows at a 45-degree angle to your torso. Avoid letting your elbows flare out too wide or hugging your body too closely.

Step 5: Controlled Descent

Lower your chest towards the ground in a controlled manner. Keep your body straight, avoiding sagging hips or a raised butt.

Step 6: Full Range of Motion

Aim to bring your chest as close to the ground as possible without touching it. Ensure your elbows are bent at a 90-degree angle at the bottom of the movement.

Step 7: Push-Up Execution

Press through your palms to straighten your arms and return to the starting position. Fully extend your elbows without locking them to complete one repetition.

Step 8: Breathing Technique

Inhale as you lower your body. Exhale as you push back up to the starting position.

Step 9: Repetitions and Sets

Start with a number of repetitions that challenge you but maintain proper form. Gradually increase the number of sets and repetitions as you build strength.
